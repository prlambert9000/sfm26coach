# SF Marathon 2026 Training Plan (v3)

**Race:** San Francisco Marathon — Sunday, July 26, 2026  
**Goal:** Sub-3:35, target 3:28–3:32. Finish healthy. Protect the knee above all else.  
**Start:** Monday, April 13, 2026  
**Duration:** 15 weeks  

---

## Guiding Principles

1. **The knee is the boss.** Any knee discomfort = 2 days off, no negotiation. If it persists beyond 48 hours, see a PT before resuming.
2. **Strength training is non-negotiable.** Lower body 2x/week for injury prevention. Upper body 2x/week (push/pull) for maintenance. Four gym sessions total.
3. **Never increase long run AND weekly mileage in the same week.** Pick one.
4. **80/20 rule.** ~80% of miles at easy/conversational pace, ~20% at tempo or faster.
5. **Soft surfaces when possible.** Sawyer Camp Trail, Bayfront paths, Rancho San Antonio — save pavement for race-specific long runs.

---

## Run Types

| Code | Type | Description |
|------|------|-------------|
| **Easy** | Easy Run | HR below 140 bpm. Fully conversational — full sentences, no effort. Pace will likely be 9:15–10:00/mi on flat ground; slower on hills. This will feel embarrassingly slow. That's correct. |
| **Tempo** | Tempo Run | "Comfortably hard." HR ~165–172 bpm. ~7:40–7:55/mi on flat. Short phrases only. This is the key workout for closing your VO2 max → marathon performance gap. |
| **Long** | Long Run | Easy HR (below 140) for the first 75%, with the option to run the last 2–4 miles at marathon pace during build/peak phases. |
| **MP** | Marathon Pace | ~8:00–8:10/mi, HR ~155–162 bpm. Your target race pace for a sub-3:32 finish. |
| **Strides** | Strides | 6–8 x 20-second accelerations to ~5K effort with full recovery between. Good for running economy. Do these on a flat, soft surface. |

Four strength sessions per week. Two pair lower + upper, two are upper-only on leg rest days:

| Day | Session | Duration |

| **Wed or Thu** | Upper Pull only | ~25 min (on rest/easy day) |

Lower body sessions always stay on Mon/Fri to keep distance from tempo (Wed) and long runs (Sun). Slot upper-only sessions into whichever day has no running or just an easy run.

During taper (weeks 13–15), reduce lower body weight but keep upper body the same.

## Lower Body Strength Plan

Two workouts per week (A and B), each ~35–40 minutes. These target the glutes, hips, and quads — the muscle groups that protect your knee. Always on Mon/Fri, away from tempo and long run days.

### Lower A: Quad & Knee Stability Focus

**Warm-up (5 min)**
- 1 min leg swings (front/back, each leg)
- 1 min leg swings (lateral, each leg)
- 1 min bodyweight squats

**Main Circuit — 3 rounds**

| Exercise | Reps | Wk 1 Start | Notes |
|----------|------|------------|-------|
| Single-leg squat to bench | 8 each leg | Bodyweight | Sit all the way down to a bench/chair, stand on one leg. This is THE exercise for runner's knee prevention. Control the descent for 3 seconds. |
| Romanian deadlift (dumbbell) | 10 each leg | 15 lbs/hand | Single-leg. Hinge at hips, slight knee bend. Builds posterior chain to balance quad dominance. |
| Step-downs | 8 each leg | Bodyweight | Stand on a step (6–8 inches). Slowly lower the opposite foot to the floor, tap, and return. 3-second descent. |
| Copenhagen plank | 20 sec each side | Bodyweight | Side plank with top leg on bench, bottom leg hanging. Builds adductors — commonly weak in runners. |
| Calf raises | 15 each leg | Bodyweight | Single-leg on a step, full range of motion (drop heel below step level). Slow on the way down. |

**Finisher — 3 sets**

| Exercise | Duration | Wk 1 Start | Notes |
|----------|----------|------------|-------|
| Wall sit | 30–45 sec | Bodyweight | Back flat against wall, thighs parallel to floor, knees at 90°. Strengthens VMO with zero shearing force on the knee. Progress to 60 sec, then single-leg. |

**Cool-down (3 min)** — Foam roll quads and IT band (30 sec each side), then pigeon stretch and calf stretch (30 sec each side). Foam rolling has better evidence for recovery than static stretching. If you're short on time, prioritize the foam rolling over the stretches.

---

### Lower B: Glute & Hip Stability Focus

**Warm-up (5 min)**
- Monster walks with mini band: 10 steps each direction
- Glute bridges: 15 reps
- 1 min walking lunges

**Main Circuit — 3 rounds**

| Exercise | Reps | Wk 1 Start | Notes |
|----------|------|------------|-------|
| Bulgarian split squat | 10 each leg | Bodyweight | Rear foot elevated on bench. Bread and butter for single-leg strength. Add dumbbells as you progress. |
| Side-lying hip abduction | 15 each side | Bodyweight | Lie on side, lift top leg 45°. Add ankle weight (2–5 lbs) when easy. Weak hip abductors are the #1 biomechanical cause of runner's knee. |
| Lateral band walks | 15 each direction | Light band | Mini band above knees, athletic stance, stay low. |
| Single-leg glute bridge | 12 each side | Bodyweight | Drive through heel, squeeze glute at top, 2-second hold. |
| Eccentric step-up | 8 each leg | Bodyweight | Step up normally, lower yourself down on one leg over 4 seconds. Use a 12–16 inch step. |

**Supplemental (every Lower session)**

| Exercise | Reps | Wk 1 Start | Notes |
|----------|------|------------|-------|
| Dead bug | 10 each side | Bodyweight | Core stability without loading the spine. Opposite arm/leg extension. |
| Pallof press | 10 each side | Light band | Anti-rotation core work with band. |

**Finisher — 3 sets**

| Exercise | Duration | Wk 1 Start | Notes |
|----------|----------|------------|-------|
| Wall sit | 30–45 sec | Bodyweight | Same as Lower A. Back flat, thighs parallel, knees at 90°. |

**Cool-down (3 min)** — Foam roll IT band and quads (30 sec each side), figure-4 stretch for piriformis (30 sec each side). Same rule: foam rolling first, stretches if you have time.

## Upper Body Strength Plan

Two push sessions, two pull sessions per week. On Mon/Fri paired with lower body. On Wed/Sat (or rest days) standalone in ~25 minutes. Goal is maintenance — keep what you have without creating fatigue that hurts running.

### Upper Push (~15 min when paired, ~25 min standalone)

**3 rounds**

| Exercise | Reps | Wk 1 Start | Notes |
|----------|------|------------|-------|
| Dumbbell bench press | 10 | 30 lbs/hand | Flat bench. Moderate weight — finish each set with 2–3 reps in reserve. Don't go to failure during marathon training. |
| Overhead press (dumbbell) | 10 | 20 lbs/hand | Standing. Engages core as a bonus. |
| Push-ups | 12–15 | Bodyweight | Slow and controlled. Elevate feet for more challenge when bodyweight gets easy. |
| Dumbbell lateral raise | 12 | 10 lbs | Light weight (8–12 lbs). Shoulder health and aesthetics. |
| Tricep dips (bench) or overhead extension | 10 | Bodyweight / 20 lbs | Bench dips or single dumbbell overhead extension. |

---

### Upper Pull (~15 min when paired, ~25 min standalone)

**3 rounds**

| Exercise | Reps | Wk 1 Start | Notes |
|----------|------|------------|-------|
| Pull-ups or lat pulldown | 8–10 | Bodyweight (or 85 lbs lat pulldown) | If you can do 8+ pull-ups, use those. Add weight via belt/dumbbell between feet when bodyweight is easy. |
| Dumbbell row | 10 each arm | 30 lbs/hand | Single-arm, knee on bench. Squeeze shoulder blade at top. |
| Face pulls (band or cable) | 15 | Light band / 20 lbs | Light resistance, high reps. Critical for shoulder health and posture — counteracts the forward-hunching of late-race marathon form. |
| Bicep curls (dumbbell) | 12 | 15 lbs/hand | Alternating or simultaneous. |
| Band pull-aparts | 15 | Light band | Light band, arms extended. Rear delt and posture work. |

## Upper Body Progression

No taper for upper work — keep the same weight and reps all 15 weeks. Pure maintenance. If you get noticeably stronger, fine, but don't chase PRs here. Save recovery capacity for running.

If a push or pull session falls on the same day as a lower session (Mon/Fri), do lower body first. Lower body is the priority.

## Knee Monitoring Protocol

**Green light (keep training):**
- No pain during or after runs
- Occasional mild stiffness that resolves within minutes of warming up

**Yellow light (modify):**
- Mild ache during runs that stays mild and doesn't worsen
- Action: Drop to easy runs only for 3–4 days. No tempo, no long run. Add extra glute/hip work. Ice after runs.

**Red light (stop running):**
- Pain that increases during a run
- Pain that persists after a run (walking, stairs)
- Any sharp or stabbing sensation
- Action: Stop running immediately. 48 hours complete rest. If not resolved, see a sports PT before resuming. Do not try to "run through it."

## Race Day Strategy

**Pacing:** Start at 8:10–8:20/mi through the hilly first half (Golden Gate Bridge, Presidio). You will be tempted to go faster. Don't. The SF course is back-loaded — the second half through Golden Gate Park is flatter and that's where you make up time. Negative split this race.

**Target splits:**
- Half: ~1:47–1:49 (deliberately conservative)
- Full: ~3:28–3:32

**Fueling:** Gel every 45 minutes starting at mile 4. Practice this in every long run over 14 miles exactly as you'll do it on race day. Same brand, same timing.

**The knee:** If the knee starts talking during the race, slow down immediately. A 3:45 finish with a healthy knee is infinitely better than a 3:28 with another injury.
